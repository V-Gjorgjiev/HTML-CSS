Learn more about our Lenses


Single Vision Lenses

Description

Single vision lenses have a uniform prescription throughout the entire lens, designed for correcting a single vision issue—either nearsightedness, farsightedness, or astigmatism.

Usage

Perfect for individuals needing glasses solely for distance vision or reading. These lenses are commonly prescribed to younger individuals without presbyopia.


Progressive Lenses

Description

Progressive lenses provide seamless vision correction at multiple distances.
The upper portion aids with distance vision, the middle with intermediate tasks, and the lower portion with near vision—eliminating the need for separate reading glasses.

Usage

Ideal for individuals over 40 with presbyopia, allowing a smooth transition between viewing distances.


Sun Lenses

Description

Designed primarily for outdoor use, sun lenses protect against intense sunlight and reduce glare for better comfort.

Usage

Perfect for outdoor activities, driving, or sports. These lenses are available with or without prescription.

Lens Colors and Features

Common colors include gray, brown, and green, with gradient colors, with mirrored finishes for enhanced glare reduction. But as the technology increases We are now able to produce every color possible making your eyewear more unique


Polarized Lenses

Description

Polarized lenses are specially designed to reduce glare caused by light reflecting off flat surfaces like water, snow, glass, or pavement.
They contain a chemical filter that blocks horizontally polarized light, which is the main contributor to glare.

Usage

Excellent for outdoor activities like driving, winter and water sports, and everyday outdoor use providing sharper vision and increased comfort.

Driving:
Cuts glare from sunlight reflecting off car hoods, windshields, and wet roads, improving safety.

Water Activities:
Ideal for fishing, boating, or beach visits, as they reduce reflections on water surfaces, allowing for better visibility beneath the surface.

Winter Sports:
Helps reduce glare from snow, which can be particularly harsh on bright, sunny days.

Everyday Outdoor Use:
Perfect for general sunglasses to provide a comfortable and strain-free experience under the sun.


Enhanced Coatings for All Lenses

Anti-Reflective (AR)

Anti-reflective coatings are designed to protect your vision from the harsh sunlight and prevent glare. They have a protective layer that blocks light from reaching the lens which minimizes reflections for clearer, more comfortable vision.

UV Protection

UV protection coatings are designed to protect your vision from the harmful UV rays that can damage your eyes. They have a protective layer that blocks UV light from reaching the lens which minimizes damage to your eyes.

Scratch-Resistant

Scratch-resistant coatings are designed to protect your vision from the harsh scratches caused by wear and tear. They have a protective layer that blocks scratches from reaching the lens which increases durability, prolonging the life of your lenses.

Blue Light Protection

Blue light protection coatings are designed to protect your vision from the harmful blue light that can damage your eyes. They have a protective layer that blocks blue light that reduces digital eye strain and promotes better sleep.

Anti-Fog

Anti-fog coatings are designed to protect your vision from the harsh fog that can build up when entering from a warm to cold enviviroment or reverse which ensures clarity in humid or cold conditions.

Good eye health goes beyond regular checkups.

Practice the '20-20-20 rule'—every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds—to reduce digital eye strain.

Common Vision Problems

Understanding Myopia, Astigmatism and More

Many people experience common vision issues like myopia (nearsightedness), hyperopia (farsightedness), and astigmatism.
These conditions can often be corrected with glasses, contact lenses, or even corrective surgery.
Talk to our optometrists about the best options for your needs.

Myopia (Nearsightedness)

Myopia, or nearsightedness, is a common condition where close objects appear clear, but distant objects appear blurry.
This happens when the eye is too long, causing light to focus in front of the retina.
Myopia can usually be corrected with prescription glasses, contact lenses, or, in some cases, laser eye surgery.

Hyperopia (Farsightedness)

Hyperopia, or farsightedness, is the opposite of myopia. People with hyperopia can see distant objects more clearly than close ones.
This occurs when the eye is too short, causing light to focus behind the retina.
Corrective options include glasses, contact lenses, and, for some, laser surgery.

Astigmatism

Astigmatism is caused by an irregularly shaped cornea or lens, which prevents light from focusing evenly on the retina.
This results in distorted or blurred vision at all distances.
Glasses or contact lenses with a special cylindrical lens power can correct astigmatism, and laser surgery is also an option.

Presbyopia

Presbyopia is an age-related condition where the eye's lens becomes less flexible, making it difficult to focus on close objects.
This typically begins around age 40 and can be corrected with reading glasses, bifocals, multifocals, or progressive lenses.
